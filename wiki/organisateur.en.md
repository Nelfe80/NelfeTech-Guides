# Tournament organizer

From the hub console (host or admin role), two tournament formats — and one golden rule: test everything **before** the event.

## ✅ Test before the event (essential)

A game is only usable if its definition exposes the **indispensable signals**: live score or records — without them, no score will ever come up. Don't find out in front of your audience:

1. **Check the signals**: "Check signals" button in the console. The verdict lists live score / records / timer.
2. **Test round**: tick "Test round" at creation — full workflow (arming, timer, scores, podium) but **nothing is recorded or published**.
3. **Replay the players' gestures**: check-in, game launch, checkout — the exact day-of journey.

## Sprint (armed round)

1. **Create the round**: game, duration, cabinets.
2. Players **launch the game on their cabinet** — the round arms itself once every reserved cabinet is ready; a cabinet on the wrong game is flagged, never forced.
3. Timer, live scores on the tournament screen, **automatic ranking** at expiry (badged players appear with their nickname), round archived.

## Open session (players take turns)

For a 1–4 hour slot on N reserved cabinets:

1. Create the round in **Open session** mode — it starts immediately.
2. Players **take turns**: each one badges in on a cabinet, plays, hands over. The current badge rules: every mark counts for its player.
3. The **per-player ranking** feeds live on the tournament screen for the whole window.
4. At the end, the podium **stays displayed** until you route the screen elsewhere.
